User interface

At the first page of an application, you can see the list of templates. Each item of the list contains name and description

Notice the arrow button. Usually description text is collapsed into the main screen. If you want to open full description, open it using the arrow button. To choose one of the templates, just click the relevant one from the list.

After you choose the template, brain images will appear on the screen

The simple page of the brain atlas viewer consists of 9 basic parts. The parts are marked with white squares. Now let's describe the parts

1,2,3 - Orthogonal 2D cross sections of the brain volume.

4 - The 3D overview of the brain volume.

5 - Logo and icons to open floating widgets such as search, layers and plugins. click for details

6 - Inside the square, you can see information about the coordinates and position of cursor. From here, it is available to reset position, rotation, and zoom for both 2D cross sections and 3D overview of brain volume. click for details

7 - Scale bar indicating current level of zoom.

8 - From this part, it is possible to select templates and parcellations. Also, it is available to search, select or deselect regions of parcellation. With that, panel contains help and login buttons. click for details

9 - With the clicking on the text, you can hide and show frontal octant in the 3D overview of the brain volume.

Let's deep dive into the regions 5, 6 and 8. These parts are complex. The items of each part could perform different functions, but mostly they are united in one category.

In the first example, the part under the number 6 is not visible well. So Let's look at it better.

As you can see, we have 4 constant and 1 temporary line

1 - Reset: from the line you can reset position rotation and zoom of coordinates.

2 - Space: from line, you can change the measure from physical to voxel.

3 - Navigation: from here you can see and change the coordinates of orthogonal 2D cross sections of the brain volume.

4 - Mouse: you can see the coordinates of the mouse.

5 - The last temporary line is visible when the cursor is hovering some region of parcellation. Accordingly, the line content is region name.
